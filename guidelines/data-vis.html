Design Guidelines

Charts are used to tell the story of the data you're presenting. You need to choose the chart that most clearly presents the main point you want to portray. Use the wrong chart type, and you risk misrepresenting the data and confusing or even misleading your audience.

  1. First, think about yourintent. What is the point of the information you want to convey?
  2. Who is your audience, and how do they prefer to consume information?
  3. Is the data used to show:
    1. Comparison: difference or similarity between different values?
    2. Trending: data that has changed over time?
