ProctorU Definitions

General Definitions

Proctor
American Usage. A supervisor especially of an examination or dormitory in a school.
Invigilator
European Usage. A supervisor especially of an examination or dormitory in a school.
Procki
The mascot for ProctorU

General User Roles

Test-taker
The person that is actually taking the test, formerly "student". Student and test-taker should not be used interchangeably since proffesional testing organizations do not identify as a student.
Organization
should only be used to define profesional testing centers
Institution
should only be used to define k12 - higher education testing centers
Prospect
Any user that doesn't curently work with ProctorU but is somewhere in the sales process
Institution User Editor
Fewest account privilages. This role gets assigned during implementation. This role can only add or edit institution/organization user accounts.
Institution Exam Administrator
User with the ability to add, upload, and edit information in ProctorU system only at exam level. This user needs to provide exam information, but not look at any other meaningful data.
Institution Instructor (Auto-Only)
User is only able to VIEW exams associated with themselves in ProctorU, but can create new exams via ProctorU Auto process.
Institution Instructor Observer
User with the ability to add, upload, and edit information in ProctorU system only at exam level. This user needs to provide exam information, but not look at any other meaningful data.

User with the ability to add, upload, and edit information in ProctorU system only at exam level. This user needs to provide exam information, but not look at any other meaningful data.

Business Intelligence Definitions

Exams Expected
Total number of test takers expected to schedule a reservation. This number is created by an admin for each exam iteration as it is entered on Archimedes.
Exams Fulfilled
Reservations that were successfully proctored. This is the total count of exams that were taken and completed by a test taker.
Exams Scheduled
Reservations scheduled for a future time. This is the total count of exams scheduled to be taken by test takers, but have not yet connected to a proctor.
Running
Total number of reservations currently in progress with a proctor. This is the total count of test taker reservations that have connected to a proctor and are currently starting or in their exam. [Internal Note: Running status is only for Live+ exams, so any institution still on Live will not have exams in Running]
Cancelled
Total number of reservations that were cancelled. This is the total count of scheduled reservations that were cancelled for any reason.
Wait Time
Amount of time spent by each test taker waiting to connect to a proctor. This time starts from the moment the test taker downloads the LMI Applet and continues until the test taker is connected with a proctor. If the test taker ends up with a greeter, that time is included here as well.
Launch Time
Amount of time each test taker worked with a proctor before entering the exam. This time starts from the moment a test taker is connected to a proctor and continues until the test taker has opened and started on the exam.
Start Time
Average time to connect to a proctor, secure testing environment, and begin the exam. This is the amount of time a test taker spends waiting to connect to a proctor, starting from the download of the LMI applet, as well as working with a proctor up to the point where they enter the exam.
Minutes in Exam
Average minutes spent in the exam by test-takers. This is the amount of time the test taker spends working on their exam. The count begins after the proctor opens the exam and continues until the proctor has verified the test taker has completed it.
Unpermitted Materials
Reservations in which unpermitted resources were identified by a proctor and removed from the testing environment. This metric only includes materials that were removed from the testing area before the test taker opens and begins to work on the exam. This is a preventative measure a proctor will take to help ensure academic integrity before the exam begins.
Prevented Breach
Number of times where a potential breach of academic integrity was prevented before it could occur. This metric is generated by a proctor when, during the exam, the test taker attempts to breach academic integrity and the behavior is stopped before that can happen.
Confirmed Breach
Total number of confirmed academic integrity breaches identified and verified by ProctorU. This metric is generated by a proctor or intervention specialist when a breach of academic integrity has occurred. This can be a one time event of egregious behavior, or a repetition of events where a prevented breach turns into a confirmed breach.
Proctor Rating
Survey score given to the Proctor by the test-taker. The scores are rated from 1 - Very Unsatisfied to 5 - Very Satisfied. This is used to calculate Proctor Satisfaction Percent.
Proctor Satisfaction Percentage
Percent of test-takers that were satisfied with their proctor. When a test taker rates their proctor at the end of their testing session, the scores are rated from 1 - Very Unsatisfied to 5 - Very Satisfied. Proctor Satisfaction Percentage is the total count of satisfied and very satisfied ratings, divided by the total number of surveys taken.
ProctorU Service Rating
Survey score given to the ProctorU service as a whole by the test-taker. The scores are rated from 1 - Very Unsatisfied to 5 - Very Satisfied. This is used to calculate ProctorU Services Satisfaction Percentage.
ProctorU Services Satisfaction Percentage
Percent of test-takers that were satisfied with their ProctorU experience. When a test taker rates their ProctorU services experience at the end of their testing session, the scores are rated from 1 - Very Unsatisfied to 5 - Very Satisfied. ProctorU Services Satisfaction Percentage is the total count of satisfied and very satisfied ratings, divided by the total number of surveys taken.
Technical Issues
Total number of reservations that experienced a technical issue. This is the count of all reservations where a technical issue occurred. They are grouped into four categories in order of severity: Connection, Audio/Video, CPU Issue, and Browser. If a test taker encounters multiple technical issues within a single fulfillment, the most severe will be the one to display.
Experienced Tech Issues
Percent of reservations that experienced a technical issue before or during the exam. This is a count of reservations that experienced a tech issue, divided by the count of exams that were connected to a proctor.
Resolved Percentage
Percent of reservations that experienced a technical issue before or during the exam and were able to complete their exam. This metric exists to show, of all sessions that encountered a technical issue, what % of them end up getting resolved and are able to complete their exam.
Completed Percentage
Percent of expected reservations that are scheduled and fulfilled. This is the sum of all reservations that are scheduled and fulfilled, divided by the expected number of test takers. It is possible for this to go over 100% if the expected number of test takers is less than the number of sessions scheduled and fulfilled.
Premium Scheduling
Whether a student paid a premium to take their exam within 72 hours of scheduling it.
Total Reservations
Total number of reservations scheduled. This is the total count of all reservations that were scheduled, fulfilled, running, and cancelled.
Completed Exams
Total number of reservations completed. This is the total count of all fulfilled reservations.
Incident Type
The distinction between an Academic Integrity breach, or a specific action that was observed and prevented. A Confirmed Breach will always be an Academic Integrity incident type, while a Prevented Breach will have a more specific incident type. This is because all Confirmed Breaches impact Academic Integrity, while a Prevented Breach was a specific action needing to be corrected that may not necessarily be an intentional breach of academic integrity. An example would be speaking aloud during the exam, but the behavior was stopped when directed by the proctor.
Breach Type
A designation between Confirmed Breach, Prevented Breach, Reviewed/No Breach.
Date
The scheduled date of the exam.
Status
The status of each appointment that was created by the test-taker.
